**Key Extraction Criteria:**

You are **NOT** just looking for random 15-60 second segments. You are searching for "gold" by identifying the *exact* moment a key concept clicks. Look for segments that meet these criteria:

3.  **The "Validating Statement:"**
    * **Direct Empathy:** A powerful, self-contained statement that speaks directly to the survivor's experience (e.g., "It was not your fault," or "You are not 'too sensitive'").
4.  **The "Relatable Question:"**
    * **Powerful Hooks:** A clip that *starts* with a deeply relatable question (e.g., "Do you ever find yourself...?"). This is the strongest hook.
5.  **Self-Contained Logic:** The clip **must make sense on its own**. It cannot start or end mid-sentence or reference a concept *not* explained within the clip. It must be a complete, coherent thought.

**What to AVOID:**
* Clips that are too academic or "in the weeds" without a clear emotional payoff.
* Clips that are dependent on the 5 minutes of content that came before it.
* Anything that sounds like a "listicle" (e.g., "The third thing is...").
